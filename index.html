Iris Multispectral Cubesat Imager

Iris is a multispectral imager that will be included on the Ex-Alta 3 cubesat mission. The imager is designed to capture image data in green (~580 nm), red (~660 nm), near-infrared (~820 nm), and short-wave infrared (SWIR) (~1600 nm).
I am a co-lead of the development team of the imager, and have worked extensively on the optomechanical design as part of the MECE 409 course offered in the fall semester. Currently the mission is at the Preliminary Design Review (PDR) stage, with an official PDR presentation scheduled for the start of March 2024.

Capstone Project: Autonomous Drone Object Retrieval

The capstone course for Engineering Physics at the University of Alberta is a two course group project that spans the fall and winter semesters of the final year. The objective of my capstone project is to develop an autonomous drone system that can take off from a submersible ROV, and retrieve an object using machine vision.
