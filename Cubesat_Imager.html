Cubesat Imager

I am currently in the process of designing a cubesat imaging payload for the University of Alberta's AlbertaSat team. This is building upon the work of the previous mission, and I am currently working on the design of the optics as well as redesigning the optomechanics. That payload is designed to fit within 1 U of the satellite with a 90 degree bend in order to accomodate a longer lightpath necessary for the optics.

Currently the main goals of the project are to increase the manufacturability of the camera by using a lens subcell design seperated by spacers. This is in contrast with the prior design of the imager which used wavesprings and retaining rings which proved to be effective but ultimately unecessary.
